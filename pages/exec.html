What is the role of the President?

The President is the primary representative of the Club to the Union and the University. They co-ordinate and oversee the actitivies of both the Executive and the Club as a whole. Acting as a chair to all club meetings. They attent the Student Actitivities (SA) AGM and other releveant meetings. As well as overseeing the safety aspects of all Club activities to ensure that the LUSU safety framework and the Club's safety code of practice is adhered to.

What is the role of the Treasurer?

The Treasurer is responsible for maintaining good order of the Club Finances and ensuring that the Union Financial Regulations are adhered to. They must maintain an up to date account of all financial transactions, advising the executive and membership on expenditure of the budget. They should also prepare a statement of revenue and expenditure to be presented at the Annual General Meeting for ratification.

What is the role of the General Secretary?

The General Secretary is responsible for organising a mailing list of Club members and other organisational activities. They are also responsible for creating and maintaining a rota for the coaching of non-BUCS training sessions. They also share responsibility for locating and maintaining sponsorship contracts with viable local businesses alongside the General Secretary. Alongside, organising entrance to NWCSL for all teams and attending the NWCSL AGM.

What is the role of the Social & Outreach Officer?

Organise and publicise a wide range of social events and activities for all members. Ensuring that the safety aspects of such activities are satisfactorily addressed and good order is maintained throughout those socials/activities. They also act as the primary contact and liaision officer for all BUCS and non-BUCS members within the Club. Endevouring to promote inclusivity and strive to develop personal connections with all members.
